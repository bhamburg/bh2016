Music

Strumming and Mumming

Oh, the places you'll go... with a banjo!

It all started when my dad first asked me to join the new Irish American String Band, take tenor banjo lessons with him, and participate in the Philadelphia New Year's Day Mummers Parade. Here we are 15 years, at least 6 banjos, and 3 different string bands later, and the banjo has taken me places that I never thought possible.

My main instrument is now the plectrum banjo, which I play as a member of the Fralinger String Band and several traditional jazz and Dixieland bands when the opportunities arise. With the Fralinger band, I've literally traveled around the world, performing in myriad parades and festivals including the International Chinese New Year Parade in Hong Kong and the Mardi Gras parades in New Orleans. As a member of Fralinger since April of 2001, I was fortunate enough to be a part of our record-setting, eight-consecutive Mummers Parade championships. It's an honor to perform with such talented musicians and represent the United States and the City of Philadelphia while entertaining people with this traditional American instrument.

Visit Fralinger.org for more information about Fralinger and the Philadelphia Mummers.

Fralinger Centennial Celebration

Branding, graphic design, and print layouts for Fralinger's 100th anniversary

In 2015 Fralinger String Band celebrated a very important milestone. One hundred years have passed since Joseph A. Ferko first led the band sponsored by Dr. John J. Fralinger in the New Year's Day Mummers Parade. As we approached the centennial celebration, I was tasked with coming up with a logo for all of our print materials and online marketing.

Examples of items that use the centennial branding include t-shirts, plastic mugs, wine glasses, a street banner, lappel pins, clubhouse signage, and most notably, the gala program. That program turned into a 150-page, full-color, 8.5 x 11" comprehensive guide to the Fralinger String Band, which I also helped organize, proofread, lay out, and design with Dan Marakowski, Jr. and others.

String Band Database

A place to look up Mummers Parade string band themes

Today I decided to revisit an old project of mine. Many a mumtape party I’ve sat there watching old string band footage wondering how the prizes came out that particular year. Usually I would need to rely on the older mums in the room to fill in the blanks, relying on their collective memory. Not anymore. Introducing String Band Database.

Backstory: We have a spreadsheet that’s been floating around among a few of us mummers that has the entire prize history of the Mummers Parade string band division. It dates back to the 1903 parade with as many details as we could recover from various sources. Using a Google Spreadsheet to run the backend, and a jQuery plugin called Sheetrock with Bootstrap on the frontend, I created this page that allows you to search the entire spreadsheet. It seems to be working pretty well, but it is in beta, so let me know if you see any issues or have any suggestions. Planned improvements include data standardization, points by subcategories, and the option to hide or show columns.

  • Date: June 2013
